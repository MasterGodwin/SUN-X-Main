Our Fleet

On-Time Delivery,
Every Single Pour

In Ready-Mix Concrete, time is concrete. The clock starts when the drum turns, and workability begins to reduce from that moment. SUN-X's fleet management system is engineered to minimise transit time and maximise delivery reliability — so your team never waits at the pump.

Each of our transit mixers is fitted with a GPS tracking unit linked to our dispatch control centre. The system monitors live truck location, drum RPM, transit duration and delivery site ETA — enabling dispatchers to intervene immediately if a truck is delayed in traffic.

Preventive maintenance schedules, drum inspection protocols and driver training programmes ensure that every truck in our fleet is fit for purpose before it loads its first batch of the day.

Fleet Operations

How We Manage Our Fleet

01
📍

Live GPS Tracking

Every transit mixer is tracked in real time from loading to discharge. Site teams receive estimated arrival updates, and dispatch can reroute trucks around traffic or site delays instantly.

02
🔧

Preventive Maintenance

Trucks follow a strict mileage-based and time-based service schedule. Critical components — drums, water pumps, hydraulic systems — are inspected and serviced before failures can occur.

03
🎓

Driver Training

All SUN-X drivers are trained in drum care, water addition protocols, discharge technique and communication with site pump operators — reducing human-error losses at the pour point.

04
⏱️

Dispatch Scheduling

Our dispatch system calculates plant-to-site transit times, batching intervals and truck turnaround cycles to ensure a steady, uninterrupted pour sequence matched to your pump output rate.

05
🚿

Drum Wash Stations

Dedicated wash-out points at each plant ensure residual concrete is fully cleared between loads. Clean drums eliminate contamination between successive mix grades on the same day.

06
📞

Site Coordination

A dedicated site liaison officer coordinates with your pump operator and pour crew to pace truck arrivals, manage waiting time, and adapt to site stoppages without wasting loaded trucks.
